What is the dividend payout ratio?
The dividend payout ratio is the part of a company's net income that is payed as dividends for common and preffered shareholdrs. Usually the higher dividend payout ratio attracts more investors to the stock and it is often expressed as a percentage.
How to calculate dividend payout ratio?
To calculate dividend payout ratio you should use the following formula:
dividend payout ratio = (dividend per share / earnings per share) * 100
Example: Company x issued $3,100,000 in dividneds in the current year with $14,500,000 in net income, calculate the payout ratio:
Using the dividend payout ratio the answer will be:
dividend payout ratio = (dividend per share / earnings per share) * 100
dividend payout ratio = ($3,100,000 / $14,500,000) * 100
dividend payout ratio = 21.38%
Why use dividend payout ratio?
Investors use the dividend payout ratio because it helps them to indicate how much money a company is returning to shareholders against how much it is keeping to reinvest in growth, pay debt or add to cash reserves.
